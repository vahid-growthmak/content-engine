## **Who Growthmak Serves**

Growthmak serves growth-ready B2B and D2C businesses — typically founder-led or owner-operated — that have achieved early revenue traction but are hitting a ceiling because their marketing infrastructure is broken, fragmented, or simply nonexistent. They are not looking for another agency to run a campaign. They need a growth partner who will diagnose the real problem and build a system to fix it.

## **2.4 Buying Trigger Events**

These are the moments that push an ICP-fit company from 'thinking about it' to 'ready to buy'. Growthmak's outreach and content should be calibrated to reach companies at these inflection points:

* Raised a funding round (Seed / Series A) — capital deployed, urgency to show traction

* Hired a new Head of Marketing, Growth, or Sales — new person wants to make an immediate impact

* Just hit a revenue plateau — strong growth year followed by a stall

* Recently fired or parted ways with a previous agency — actively searching for a better partner

* Expanding to a new geography (e.g., Indian company launching in UAE or USA)

* Launching a new product line or pivoting the offer — needs new acquisition infrastructure

* Lost a major client and needs to rebuild pipeline urgently

* Preparing for a fundraise and needs to show marketing momentum to investors

* E-commerce brand moving from marketplace (Amazon/Flipkart) to direct-to-consumer

| Too early-stage | Pre-product businesses with no revenue, no validated offer, or no budget whatsoever |
| :---- | :---- |

| **Purely B2C mass-market** | High-volume, low-margin consumer brands with no analytical foundation (e.g., FMCG at scale) |

# **Section 3: Buyer Personas**

Three distinct personas cover the human archetypes Growthmak encounters most frequently. All three exist within the ICP companies defined above. Teams should use these personas to tailor messaging, content, outreach, and proposals.

## **Persona 1: The Growth-Stuck Founder**

***Primary Decision-Maker — Highest Frequency — Highest Deal Value***

| Fictional Name | Arjun Mehta / Alex Carter / Aisha Al-Farsi |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Age** | 28 to 42 |
| **Title** | Founder / Co-Founder / Managing Director |
| **Education** | Bachelor's or MBA; often in engineering, business, or commerce |
| **Income/Revenue** | Company revenue: $1M to $8M; personal income significant but reinvesting into business |
| **Location** | Bengaluru, Mumbai, Dubai, Singapore, London, or remote in USA |

**A Day in Their Life**

Arjun wakes up at 7am and immediately checks his phone — WhatsApp messages from his team, a Slack notification about a client issue, two unread emails from prospects who went cold. He's managing twelve things before breakfast. By 9am he's in a stand-up with his 15-person team. The rest of his day is split between client delivery, sales calls, team firefighting, and a brief window in the evening where he tries to think strategically.

He knows he needs to spend more time on marketing. He's been saying it for six months. Every week, something more urgent takes over. The company has grown mostly from referrals — which feels both gratifying and terrifying. What happens when the referrals dry up? He doesn't have an answer. The anxiety shows up at 11pm when he can't sleep.

He uses LinkedIn daily but mostly consumes content — rarely posts because he doesn't know what to say or doesn't have time. He Googles things like 'how to get more B2B leads' and 'what's a good CPC for LinkedIn ads' and finds himself reading agency blogs for 20 minutes before realizing he's procrastinating. He uses Google Workspace, Slack, Notion, HubSpot (underused), and a WhatsApp group that's effectively his CRM.

**Goals & Motivations**

* Build a predictable, scalable pipeline that does not depend on his personal network

* Grow revenue by 2-3x within 12 months without proportionally scaling headcount

* Free himself from day-to-day sales so he can focus on product and team

* Be seen as the credible leader of a serious, professional business — not a freelancer with a logo

* Eventually attract investment or build toward an exit at a strong valuation

**Pains & Frustrations**

* Marketing feels like a black box — he spends money, doesn't know what returns

* Previous agency ran ads for 3 months, reported impressions and clicks, couldn't tie it to revenue

* His website 'looks fine' but he has no idea how many people visit it, what they do, or why they don't convert

* He feels like he should be doing LinkedIn content but doesn't know where to start and dreads sounding generic

* His team is stretched — there's no dedicated marketing person, so everything falls on him or gets deprioritized

* He's been burned before: paid for a cheap website, paid for SEO that 'didn't work', tried running Meta ads himself and lost money

**Fears & Objections**

* 'Another agency will overpromise and underdeliver — I've been there.'

* 'I can't commit to a long-term retainer if I don't know I'll see results.'

* 'Is Growthmak big enough / experienced enough to handle my specific industry?'

* 'My business is different from what they usually work with — will they really understand it?'

* 'What if I invest $5k/month and nothing changes?'

**Decision-Making Process**

| Discovery | Google search, LinkedIn recommendations, peer referrals from founder communities |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Evaluation** | Reads the website carefully, looks at case studies, checks Clutch/LinkedIn reviews, asks peers |
| **Decision Trigger** | Sees a case study that mirrors his situation, or gets a referral from someone he trusts |
| **Buying Committee** | Usually decides alone, sometimes consults co-founder or Head of Marketing |
| **Sales Cycle** | 2 to 6 weeks from first contact to signed proposal |
| **What Closes the Deal** | Honesty in the diagnostic call, clear prescription, realistic expectations, proof of past work |

**Language — How He Talks About His Problem**

* 'We're growing on referrals but I can't scale referrals.'

* 'Our ads aren't working — we spent X and got nothing.'

* 'I don't know which part of our marketing is actually working.'

* 'I need someone who actually understands B2B, not just someone who runs campaigns.'

* 'We tried SEO but nothing happened after 6 months.'

* 'I want proper tracking so I can actually see the ROI.'

**Brand & Tool Universe**

| Aspirational Brands | Stripe, Notion, Figma, Linear — admires clean design, opinionated products, clear value |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Marketing Tools Used** | Google Ads (dabbled), Meta Ads Manager (basic), Mailchimp or Brevo, Canva |
| **Productivity Stack** | Notion, Slack, Google Workspace, Zoom, Calendly |
| **Media Consumed** | LinkedIn articles, Y Combinator blog, First Round Review, Marketing Examples, podcasts: Founders, How I Built This |
| **Communities** | Startup India forums, YC alumni communities, Founder LinkedIn groups, local startup ecosystem events |
| **Influencers/Thought Leaders** | Follows marketing educators on LinkedIn; respects honest, data-driven voices over hype-filled 'gurus' |
| **Events Attended** | SaaS conferences, local startup meetups, marketing summits in metro cities |

**Competitor Relationship**

* Has likely tried 1-2 agencies before — usually a full-service agency or cheap freelancer

* Loves: agencies that communicate clearly, report with real numbers, explain what they did and why

* Hates: vanity metrics, weekly reports full of graphs that say nothing about revenue impact

* Gap he feels competitors leave: no one shows him the full picture — they just run the thing they were hired to run

* Key objection to competitors: 'They just execute tasks. Nobody thinks about our growth holistically.'

Priya is the first marketing hire at a 30-person B2B SaaS company. Her founder hired her six months ago to 'sort out marketing.' She inherited no documented strategy, a broken website, and a Google Ads account that hasn't been touched in a year. She's smart, motivated, and completely overwhelmed.

She spends most of her day in Slack, fighting fires, writing ad copy, updating the website, preparing monthly reports for the CEO, and trying to figure out why their LinkedIn impressions aren't converting to leads. She knows enough to know she needs help — specifically, someone who can own the technical side (ads, tracking, CRO) so she can focus on content and brand.

She evaluates Growthmak differently than the Founder does — she's thinking about whether the agency will make her look good to her CEO, whether they'll be easy to manage, and whether they'll fill the exact skill gaps she has.

**Goals & Motivations**

**Pains & Frustrations**

* She's expected to do everything: ads, content, SEO, social, email — with a team of one (herself)

* The CEO asks for numbers she can't produce because the tracking is broken

* Agencies she's approached either want to own everything (and lock her out) or just 'consult' without doing any actual work

* She can't get internal developer support to fix the tracking issues on the site

* She's nervous about spending budget on things she can't report on confidently

**Fears & Objections**

* 'If the agency underperforms, it reflects on me — I recommended them.'

* 'Will they be transparent with reporting or hide behind vanity metrics?'

* 'Are they going to work with me as a partner or just around me?'

# **Section 3B: Competitor Intelligence Summary**

This section maps the competitive landscape Growthmak operates in and how to position effectively against each type of competitor. Based on the brand's repositioning to a diagnostic-led, full-engine model, these are the most common alternatives a prospect is evaluating:

| Competitor Type | Their ICP | What Customers Like | What Customers Hate | Growthmak Advantage |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Full-service agency (mid-size) | Any B2B or B2C, $5K-$50K/mo | Brand recognition, wide team, multiple services under one roof | Expensive, slow, account managers who don't understand the work, generic strategies | Founder-close, diagnostic-first, Growth Engine framing, clear accountability |
| Freelancer / Solopreneur | Budget-conscious SMB, simple executional tasks | Cheap, fast, personal attention | Limited scope, unreliable, no strategic thinking, disappears mid-project | Full team, system-level thinking, reliable delivery, documented process |
| Performance marketing agency | Companies spending $10K+/mo on ads | Ad management expertise, media buying scale | Only cares about ad metrics, no CRO/Foundation context, % of spend pricing \= conflict of interest | Flat fee (no % of spend), Foundation-first approach, tied to revenue not impressions |
| In-house marketing hire | Companies that want control and prefer staff over agency | Full-time focus, brand knowledge, responsiveness | Hard to hire well, slow to ramp, skill gaps in technical areas, expensive all-in | Faster ramp, full-stack expertise, no hiring risk, no benefits/equity cost |
| Content/SEO agency | Companies focused on organic growth | Content volume, SEO rankings over time | Slow results, no paid acquisition integration, often disconnected from business outcomes | Integrated approach: content \+ ads \+ CRO \+ ops in one engine |

**Key Competitive Gaps Growthmak Fills**

* No competitor consistently leads with a diagnostic-first model — most sell before they understand the problem

* Growthmak's 5-layer Growth Engine framework gives prospects a mental model no generic agency offers

* Flat-fee pricing on ads removes the % of spend conflict of interest that frustrates sophisticated founders

* The combination of Foundation \+ Traffic \+ Conversion in one integrated system is rare — most agencies only own one layer
