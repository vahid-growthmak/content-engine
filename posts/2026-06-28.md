## POST COPY

If you can't answer these 3 questions before your AI pilot launches, you're not building a competitive advantage.

You're building expensive theatre.

BCG, MIT, and RAND all studied enterprise AI adoption.

The verdict? 95% of AI pilots fail to produce measurable P&L impact.

Not because the tools don't work.

Because the wrong problem was chosen to begin with.

Here's what I see most founders do:

→ Competitor launches an AI chatbot → they launch one too
→ Board asks "what's our AI strategy?" → they sign 3 SaaS contracts in 30 days
→ Conference speaker hypes AI content automation → they automate their worst-performing content, just faster

That's not a strategy. That's social proof anxiety.

The 5% of companies that actually see ROI from AI do one thing differently:

They answer 3 questions BEFORE they touch a single vendor demo.

**Question 1: What specific workflow is broken enough that fixing it moves a P&L line?**

Not "we want to save time." Not "we want to scale."

A P&L line. CAC. Sales cycle. Support cost. Churn rate. Pick one.

If you can't name it, your AI pilot doesn't have a job to do.

**Question 2: What does "good" look like — in numbers — at 90 days?**

If you can't define the target before you start, you can't measure it after.

And if you can't measure it, your CFO will kill it. Probably correctly.

**Question 3: Who inside the business owns the outcome?**

Not the vendor. Not IT. Not "the team."

A named person. Whose performance review includes this metric.

If you don't have clean answers to all three — no AI tool will save you.

Garbage in. Garbage out. Just with a cleaner UI and a bigger invoice.

I've helped B2B SaaS, e-commerce, and professional services founders actually extract P&L value from AI.

It always starts with this clarity work — not the tech stack.

Are you building AI on top of a solid foundation — or just automating your way to the same dead end?

Drop your honest answer below.
