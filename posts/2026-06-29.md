## FINAL POST

Unpopular opinion: Your lead problem isn't a lead problem.

It's a system problem.

I've had the same conversation with 20+ founders this year.

Different industries. Different markets. Different budgets.

But the script is identical:

"We need more leads."

So I ask: Where are the leads you're already getting going?

Silence.

---

Here's what's actually happening inside a broken growth system:

**Problem 1: No tracking.**
You can't see where leads come from. You don't know which channel works. So you guess — and you double down on the wrong thing while cutting what's quietly working.

**Problem 2: No nurture.**
A lead comes in. Gets one email. Doesn't respond. Gets dropped.

That founder was 90% ready to buy. They just needed two more touchpoints. You ghosted them first.

**Problem 3: No ICP clarity.**
Your ads, your content, your landing page — they speak to everyone.

Which means they speak to no one.

You're filling the top of the funnel with people who will never convert. Then wondering why conversion rates are low.

---

Here's what this actually costs:

Last year we ran a diagnostic on a B2B SaaS company spending ₹6L/month on LinkedIn ads.

Getting leads. Not closing.

We paused the ads. Fixed the tracking. Tightened the ICP. Built a 5-step nurture sequence.

Turned the ads back on at 70% of the original budget.

Pipeline doubled in 60 days.

The lead volume didn't change. The system did.

---

Before you spend another rupee on ads, answer these three questions honestly:

→ Do you know where each lead comes from — specifically?
→ Do you have a nurture sequence running without you?
→ Are you targeting a defined ICP — or "anyone who might buy"?

If even one answer is "no" — that's your growth ceiling.

More leads won't lift it. Fixing the system will.
